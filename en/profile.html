Born in Fukuoka City, Fukuoka. In 2009, she enrolled in the Takarazuka Music School. In 2011, she joined the Takarazuka Revue as a member of the 97th class (ranked 7th upon admission). She made her debut on the Star Troupe production "Nova Bossa Nova / Meguriai wa Futatabi."

In 2012, she was assigned to the Snow Troupe. She appeared in numerous productions, building her experience as a musumeyaku (female role performer). On August 15, 2016, she retired from the Takarazuka Revue with the final performance of "Roman Holiday."

After leaving Takarazuka, she has continued to be active across a wide range of fields, including stage, film, MC hosting, and lectures. In 2019, she appeared in the musical "Elisabeth" at the Imperial Theatre. In 2020, she appeared as Miss Sendai in the NHK morning drama "Yell." In 2024, she appeared in "The Rose of Versailles 50: Half a Century of Legacy." Drawing on the expressive power and elegance cultivated at Takarazuka, she continues to shine as a truly one-of-a-kind performer.

Additionally, after leaving Takarazuka, she passed the Certificate for Students Achieving the Proficiency Level of Upper Secondary School Graduates. In 2017, she enrolled in the Faculty of Economics at Keio University (correspondence course). After eight and a half years of study, she graduated in March 2026. Her dedication to balancing both her performing career and academic pursuits has drawn widespread admiration.
